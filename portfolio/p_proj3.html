Interactive Dumpling Making Recipe

Welcome to my senior project!

I did this project when I was an Integrated Design and Media senior at New York University. It aimed to mitigate people's homesickness by providing interactive animated cooking tutorials using augmented reality.

This is an animated interactive recipe toturial using augmented reality (AR).

It is designed to entertain and facilitate people who live away from home, and would like to make their home-made food themselves.

Ideation


The inspiration I've had for this project is the total failure I came across when I was tryingt to make dumplings on the lunar new year eve (shown in the video).

It occurred to me that there might be a problem where people away from home being unable to make their home food. The tutorial online isn't always same as their family versions. That being said, having a "family-taught" tutorial would be helpful.

The first recipe in this series is dumpling making from my mom.

After taking 3D Modeling, 3D Animation and 3D for AR, and due to my passion about animated movies, I've decided to use those techniques to build a useful, interactive and fun cooking toturial series.

Involving AR


Augmented reality facilitates the interactivity of this project.

There are two phases I've got through with AR. I mainly use Lens Studio (by SnapChat) at first, and did a couple of user testing.

However, the Lens Studio doesn't allow users to use both their hands, because one has to hold a device. Also, when the animation is done, the file has been too big for Lens Studio.

Luckily, digital artist Carla Gannis and my AR professor Mark Skwarek gave me this idea of Hololens, which is a pair of AR glasses connected to Unity. In this case, the hands are free so that the interactivity will be brought to the next level.
